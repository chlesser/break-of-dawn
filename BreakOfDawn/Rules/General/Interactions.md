## Interactions
Certain classes can interact with certain objects that are within their fields. The GM will decide what field a certain interaction is in. These fields are as follows:

**Agility**   
Agility concerns reflexes, manual dexterity, and acrobatic acumen. Agility interactions could be made for a number of circumstances, such as attempting to jump a great height or distance, to extricate oneself from restraints, pick a mechanical lock, or similar tasks. 

**Crafting**  
Crafting interactions can be used to make and repair technology or other mundane devices. Crafting can encompass tasks as complex as repairing an engine or as simple as shaping a stone axe. While not as useful in combat as other interactions, a creative Spartan can make great use of this skill while not in the throes of battle. 

**Charisma**  
Charisma interactions are made to convince, haggle, deceive, negotiate, or otherwise alter the attitudes of another creature.

**Demolition**  
Demolition interactions involve the understanding, identification, maintenance, and use of explosive devices such as mines, grenades, artillery shells, and more. Demolition interactions may be required to properly use, set, or detonate certain explosive devices. 

**Detection**   
Detection interactions pertain to spatial awareness. Detection interactions are necessary when attempting to locate a creature or object, or notice visual, olfactory, or auditory stimuli. Detection interactions may also be necessary when attempting to discern a creature or object that is hidden or obscured. 

**Firepower**   
Firepower interactions encompass a mechanical and practical understanding of firearms and related weaponry such as energy weapons, kinetic weapons, large-scale weapons such as MAC-Cannons and artillery batteries. Firepower interactions could be used to identify, maintain, and modify such weaponry. 

**Fortitude**  
Stamina, hardiness, and endurance are all represented by Fortitude. Fortitude interactions may be called for to resist debilitating effects such as poisons, harsh weather, extreme temperatures, or physically demanding tasks. 

**Handling**  
When attempting to manipulate or control vehicles, you may be required to make a Handling interaction. This could be for calming a riding horse or trying to jump a large gap with a warthog.

**History**   
When attempting to recall a fact from earlier times, or any other piece of knowledge that took place in the past, you may need to roll a history check. This can encompass such things as names, dates and basic facts, but also more specific things such as recent military Operations or newsworthy events.

**Lifting**  
Many activities that involve general strength will require a lifting roll. This can include lifting, moving and pulling heavy objects, but it can also be used for combat maneuvers like grappling.

**Medicinal**   
Recalling and utilizing medical knowledge falls under this interaction. Both an understanding of medicine, such as anatomy, biochemistry, psychology, etc, and medical skills such as first aid, administering medpacs, creating medicinal items, etc. would fall in this field. 

**Stealth**   
When attempting to go unnoticed, a Stealth interaction will be necessary. Stealth interactions involve obscuring oneself, whether moving, stationary, or attempting to keep actions hidden from observers. 

**Tactics**  
Tactics interactions involve observing and analyzing the battlefield, as well as using this analysis to create strategies to more effectively work with other members of your fireteam. Tactic interactions may involve identifying adequate cover, anticipating an enemy’s next move, coordinating with teammates, or navigating battlefield hazards.

**Tech**   
Any interactions that involve attempting to use, decipher, or identify a technological device require a Tech interaction. 

**Tracking**   
Tracking encompasses all skills used to follow a creature that you have lost sight of or have only evidence of such as footprints, blood trails, or particularly strong scents. The difficulty of these checks may vary over different types of terrain such as mud, snow, or stone.

Interactions are based on the tier of difficulty of the interaction. These tiers are:

Easy- DC 40  
Mediocre- DC 65  
Medium- DC 80  
Hard- DC 105  
Extremely Challenging- DC 130  
Close to impossible- DC 150

A roll of 100 on any of these is an immediate success, while a 1 is an immediate fail. At the GM’s discretion, rolling a 100 may be considered a “critical success” and provide additional benefits, or rolling a 1 may be considered a “critical failure” and invoke additional penalties. 

Improving Interactions  
At certain levels, you may have the opportunity to gain “Interaction Points.” The Spartan can distribute these Interaction Points across their Interactions, with each point giving a permanent \+1 bonus to that Interaction. Additionally, as you rank up, the bonus to Interactions that you have superiority or expertise in will increase.

Interaction Contests  
Certain game instances may involve an interaction contest between two creatures. When this happens, both creatures must perform a given interaction and compare their result, adding any applicable modifiers. The creature with the higher total wins the contest, with ties being won by the targeted creature.
