## Vehicle Statistics
Vehicles are larger than people, and will have a harder time avoiding bullet fire. The severity of this penalty is directly proportional to the size of the vehicle. The table below lists the relative penalties of vehicle sizes. If you are in a vehicle when it is reduced to 0 health, you take a certain number of damage, equal to the damage dice listed on the table below.

**Vehicle Dodge Penalties:**
Small: -5
Medium: -10
Large: -15
Massive: -20

**Vehicle Sizes**
Small: 5x5 feet
Medium: 10x10 feet
Large: 15x15 feet
Massive: 20x20 feet or larger

### Vehicle Destruction Damage
When a vehicle is destroyed, creatures inside and near the vehicle will take a large amount of damage as the vehicle explodes around them. Each vehicle has a different amount of damage, Dodge DC and radius of explosion. Creatures inside the vehicle take full damage on a failed dodge, and half damage (rounded down) on a success. All creatures in the radius take half damage (rounded down) on a failed dodge and no damage on a success. The different stats for each size are as follows:

**Small:**  
Damage: 5d12
Dodge DC: 65
Radius: 5 feet

**Medium:** 
Damage: 10d12
Dodge DC: 75
Radius: 10 feet

**Large:** 
Damage: 15d12
Dodge DC: 85
Radius: 10 feet

**Massive:** 
Damage: 20d12
Dodge DC: 95
Radius: 15 feet


**Vehicle Damage Reduction**
Since metal is more durable than flesh, vehicles will not take as much damage as normal creatures. All non-explosive damage that is not dealt by another vehicle is halved.


### Statistics
**Health:** The amount of damage a vehicle can take before its destruction. A vehicle’s health is divided between the health of its chassis and the health of its component parts. A vehicle’s components are listed in its stat block. By default, all damage sustained by the vehicle is dealt to its chassis, but the attacker may choose to attack a different component of the vehicle, which damages that component as well as the chassis, potentially disabling that part of the vehicle. Vehicle components as well as their disabled effects are listed below. 

**Shields:** The amount of shields a vehicle has. The shields apply to all components of the vehicle and are lost regardless of which component is damaged.

**Speed:** How fast the vehicle can move in a turn.

**Terrain:** Where the vehicle operates (e.g., a helicopter’s terrain would be the air).

**Size:** How large the vehicle is.

**Seats:** The number of creatures that can fit in a vehicle. It is divided into pilots, gunners, turrets and passengers. Pilots are the number of people operating and driving the vehicle. Gunners are the number of people that can fire their weapon from the vehicle. Turrets are the number of people that can operate the turrets, and passengers are the number of people that ride in the vehicle without doing anything significant.

**Turret Type:** The type of turret that is on a vehicle.

**Ram Damage:** The amount of damage done if you ram someone with the vehicle.

**Ram Dodge:** The Dodge DC for the ram.

**Weapon Damage:** The amount of damage the vehicle’s weapon does.

**Weapon Dodge:** The Dodge DC of the vehicle.

**Weapon Type:** The type of damage the weapon does.

**Attacks in a Turn:** The number of attacks the vehicle can take in a single Operation.

**Weapon Range:** The range of the vehicle’s weapon.

**Special Abilities:** Any abilities the vehicle has that aren’t universal among all vehicles.


### Vehicle Components
When making an attack against different vehicle components, you may add the called shot bonus of the weapon to your attack, however the bonus granted cannot bring the penalty to more than -0. Additionally, any abilities that allow you to target 2 or more targets, such as Split or Twice the Pride, can allow you to target multiple components according to how many targets you may attack. 

**Axles:** For vehicles that do not rely on flight as their Primary means of locomotion, they are propelled by either treads or tires, which are in turn driven by axles. In this context, the component of Axles refers rather to the tires or treads themselves as opposed to the actual drive shaft(s). 
Dodge DC Penalty: -10
Disable Effect: When a vehicle’s axles are disabled the movement of that vehicle is reduced to 0.

**Cockpit:** Some vehicles provide extra protection for their pilots in the form of a cockpit. Cockpits are enclosed metal and/or glass structures that surround the pilot, shielding them from harm. 
Dodge DC Penalty: -10
Disable Effect: When a vehicle’s cockpit is disabled, the vehicle’s pilot becomes exposed and can be attacked directly with the same -10 Dodge DC penalty. In addition, once the vehicle's cockpit is disabled, or if it doesn’t have a cockpit, the vehicle can be hijacked. 

**Engine:** A vehicle’s engine is its Primary driving force. An engine is usually contained within the vehicle itself but often parts such as thrusters, exhausts, and other parts are exposed. 
Dodge DC Penalty: -25
Disable Effect: When a vehicle’s engine is disabled, roll a d10. On a 1, the vehicle explodes, and is destroyed, triggering the necessary damage roll for vehicle destruction. Otherwise, the vehicle’s speed is halved (rounded down) and its dodge penalty is doubled.

**Turrets:** In addition to built-in weaponry, some vehicles also possess a turret that can be operated by someone other than the pilot. The most common types are chaingun turrets and plasma cannons, but can include any other type of turret, such as gauss turrets. If a vehicle has multiple turrets, then each turret must be disabled individually.
Dodge DC Penalty: -5
Disable Effect: When a vehicle’s turret is disabled, the turret can no longer be used to make attacks. 

**Wings:** Flying vehicles are kept aloft by their wings. A vehicle with this component may not have wings in the traditional sense, but instead rotors, repulsors, or thrusters that serve a similar function. 
Dodge DC Penalty: -15
Disable Effect: When a vehicle’s wings are disabled, its speed is halved (rounded down) and its dodge penalty is doubled. In addition, on the start of each of the pilot’s turns, roll a d6. On a 1, if the vehicle has not landed, it crashes to the ground, with both vehicle and pilot taking 6 (1d10) of damage for every 10 feet fallen, in a random location determined by the GM within the vehicle’s maximum speed. All creatures in the vicinity of the crash (GM’s discretion) are treated as if they had been targeted by the vehicle’s ram attack and must make relevant dodge rolls to avoid the associated damage. 

**Weapons Systems:** Vehicles equipped for war often have built-in weapons and artillery that are operated by the vehicle’s pilot. This could include such weapons as a tank’s cannon or a falcon’s machine guns. 
Dodge DC Penalty: -20
Disable Effect: When a vehicle’s weapons systems are disabled, the vehicle’s pilot loses 
the ability to make attacks with the vehicle’s weapons.

### Hijacking
In many situations a player or creature may attempt to hijack a vehicle. Hijacking involves a creature outside of a vehicle attempting to forcefully remove one of the vehicle’s pilots. In order to do this, the creature attempting to hijack the vehicle must be within 5 feet of the vehicle and make an Agility or Handling interaction as a Primary Operation to climb onto the vehicle, followed by a Lifting check, also as a Primary Operation, to pull one pilot out of the vehicle, contested by another Lifting check made by the pilot. If the creature attempting the Hijack succeeds, the Pilot is pulled from the vehicle and falls to the ground to any unoccupied space within 5 feet of the vehicle and is exposed to the Fallen effect. If the creature attempting the hijack fails, they must reroll their Agility interaction to continue to hold on the vehicle or fall to the ground into an unoccupied space within 5 feet of the vehicle and be exposed to the Fallen effect. The DC for the Agility interaction is typically determined by the size of the vehicle (see the table below), modified by an additional +10 if the vehicle is flying.

**Small:** DC 90
**Medium:** DC 75
**Large:** DC 60
**Massive:** DC 45
(These values can be altered at the GM’s discretion)

  
  

### Vehicle Operations
While driving a vehicle, you cannot make Combat Maneuvers (unless otherwise stated by the vehicle’s Stat Block), but you gain access to a new set of options for your Operation. They are as follows:

**Entering/Exiting a Vehicle:** Primary Operation and all remaining movement speed with a minimum of 5 feet

**Using the Vehicle’s Weapons:** Primary Operation

**Ram:** Secondary Operation (provided that you have moved at least 20 feet towards the target in that turn)

**Moving the Vehicle:** Tertiary Operation

**Ram**
When you successfully hit another vehicle with a Ram attack, your own vehicle takes damage equal to half of the damage dealt (rounded down). 

**Combat Maneuvers**
Unless otherwise stated in the vehicle’s stat block, vehicles are not capable of using any combat maneuvers.

**Effects Immunity**
Unless otherwise stated, all vehicles are immune to the Blinded, Deafened, Frightened, Grappled, Indomitable, Poisoned, and Unconscious effects.

**Stationary**
When a vehicle is not being piloted, it is stationary and suffers a -25 to all dodges.
