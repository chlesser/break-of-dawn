# Effects
**Alert:** Any creature exposed to the Alert effect has a \+25 to dodges and Detection interactions.  
**Blinded:** Any creature exposed to the Blinded effect is unable to see and as a result suffers a \-30 penalty to all dodge rolls as well as a \-15 penalty to the Dodge DCs of any attacks they make while blinded. Additionally, if the creature moves more than 10 feet in a single turn while blinded, it must succeed on a DC 45 Agility interaction or become exposed to the Fallen effect.   
**Deafened:** Any creature exposed to this cannot hear and automatically fails any actions that rely on hearing.   
**Fallen:** Any creature exposed to this effect will be knocked down, and have a \-20 to all dodges. Any creature exposed to the Fallen effect must expend half of their movement (rounded down) on their turn to end this effect. If you choose to move without ending this effect, treat all movement as difficult terrain.  
**Frightened:** Any creature exposed to the Frightened effect must roll a d4 at the beginning of their turn. Depending on the roll of the dice, the affected creature is exposed to a random outcome.

1. The Frightened creature must spend all of its Movement speed to move away from the creature or object it is frightened by.  
2. The Frightened creature’s Movement speed is reduced to 0\.  
3. The Frightened creature must attack the creature that frightened it. Any attacks made by the Frightened creature have a \-15 penalty to Dodge DCs.  
4. The creature loses all Operations.

**Grappled:** Any creature exposed to the Grappled effect has their speed set to 0, and exposed to the Incapacitated effect.  
**Incapacitated:** Any creature exposed to the Incapacitated effect has a \-35 to dodges, and any attacks made by an Incapacitated creature have their Dodge DC reduced by \-15. Incapacitated creatures must spend a Primary Operation to move any distance.  
**Indomitable:** Any creature exposed to this effect gains the following benefits.   
\-Add \+15 to dodge  
\-Add \+10 feet of movement speed  
\-Extra Secondary and Tertiary Operation  
\-Add \+2 damage to all attacks  
**Invisible:** Any creature exposed to the Invisible effect gains an additional \+20 bonus to all dodges and Stealth interactions. This bonus to dodge and Stealth interactions is reduced by \-5 every time the creature takes damage while exposed to the Invisible effect. When the bonus to dodge and Stealth interactions is reduced to 0, the effect immediately ends.   
**Poisoned:** Make a Fortitude interaction with a DC determined by the source of the effect. If the interaction fails, the creature is exposed to the Poisoned effect. While this effect lasts, the creature suffers a \-5 penalty to dodge rolls. In addition, when exposed to the Poisoned effect, the creature may only make half as many attacks (rounded down) with your weapons per turn. If this would cause a weapon to have less than one attack per turn, then that weapon requires forfeiture of your next Primary Operation to attack with, effectively making that weapon only able to be used once every other round until the effect ends. At the start of each of your turns, you may repeat the Fortitude interaction, ending the effect on a success.   
**Stunned:** Any creature exposed to the Stunned effect is rendered completely immobile, losing all of its movement speed, and suffers a \-40 to all dodges. The creature also cannot take any Operations.  
**Surprised:** A surprised creature has a \-25 to dodges made against creatures it is unaware of.  
**Unconscious:** Any creature exposed to the Unconscious effect is unable to take any Operations, and is exposed to the Incapacitated effect. An ally using a Primary Operation to revive the affected creature ending both the Unconscious and Incapacitated effects.  
**Wounded:** Any creature exposed to the Wounded effect has their movement speed halved (rounded down) and has \-10 to all dodges. This effect lasts until the health of the creature is healed in any way. If a creature exposed to the Wounded effect goes Unconscious they gain a tier of Exhaustion.
