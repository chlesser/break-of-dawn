## ALLY AND ENEMY STATISTICS

In your missions as a fireteam, you will likely encounter many creatures, both hostile and friendly. Some will aid you in battle, but most will stand in the way to your objective. The statistics for these creatures is listed below.

Creature Sizes  
Small: Smaller than 5x5 feet  
Medium: 5x5 feet  
Large: 10x10 feet  
Massive: 15x15 feet or larger

These sizes are accounting for a grid where each square is 5x5 feet.

Statistics Guide  
Creature’s Name (Classification)

| Core | Offensive | Support |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Race:** The creature’s race. **Classification:** The creature’s classification or rank within their race. **Health:** The amount of health a creature has. **Shields:** The amount of shields a creature has. **Dodge Bonus:** The creature’s dodge bonus. **Size:** The creature’s size **Speed:** The creature’s speed. This will also designate whether or not the creature can fly. | **Melee Dodge DC:** The creature’s Dodge DC of their melee attack **Melee Damage:** The damage of the creature’s melee attack on a hit **Grenades:** The number and type of grenades the creature has. **Available Weaponry:** The weapons that the creature has access to. It is recommended that each creature only has 2 or 3 weapons equipped at a time, regardless of how many may be available to them.  | **Superiorities (+?):** The creature’s interaction Superiorities and Superiority bonus. **Expertises (+?):** The creature’s interaction Expertises and Expertise bonus. **Weapon Superiorities:** The creature’s Weapon Superiorities. **Weapon Expertises:** The Creature’s Weapon Expertises. **Effect Immunities:** The Effects that the creature is immune to. |
| **Innate Skills** | **Passive Skills** | **Operational Skills** |
| Innate Skills are abilities tied to the creature’s race or classification. | Passive Skills are non-Innate abilities that do not require the use of any Operations. | Operational skills are non-Innate abilities that require the use of Operations |

Combat Maneuvers  
Unless otherwise stated in the creature’s stat block, creatures are not capable of using any combat maneuvers.

Weapon Superiority and Expertise  
Many creatures have superiority or expertise in certain weapons and will thus be significantly more efficient with them. The bonuses they get are dependent on the size of the weapon and are as follows:

**Weapon Superiority**  
Small/Medium: \+1 damage die, \+5 Dodge DC  
Large/Massive: \+2 damage die, \+10 Dodge DC

**Weapon Expertise**  
Small/Medium: \+3 damage die, \+10 Dodge DC  
Large/Massive: \+4 damage die, \+20 Dodge DC

## UNSC ALLIES

Ally Combat  
At the GM’s discretion, at least three allies within 30 feet of each other can be organized into squads, meaning that so long as they are within 30 feet of one other ally that is part of that squad, they are counted as a single creature for purposes of initiative, movement, and combat. Before initiative is rolled, the GM may designate a group of allies within this distance to be considered a squad and will roll initiative as if they were a single creature, with no bonuses. When this group is formed, their health is calculated as a unit and is equal to the sum of the health of each of the collective members. Whenever the squad loses an amount of health equal to the squad’s maximum health divided by the number of members, a single ally is removed from the squad. If a squad is reduced to two or fewer members it ceases to be counted as a squad and is counted as individual creatures operating on the same initiative count. On its turn, the squad does not take Operations as normal but instead, deals an amount of damage to any creature of its choice within 50 feet equal to the current health of the squad divided by 4\. The squad has no bonuses to dodges, attacks, or any dice rolls made as a squad.
